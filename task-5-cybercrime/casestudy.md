# Case Study: Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying is a form of online harassment where individuals use digital platforms to intimidate, shame or harm others.
It commonly occurs on social media, messaging apps or gaming platforms.
A well known real life example is the case of Megan Meier a teenager who was targeted through a fake online profile.

Typically cyberbullying begins when a bully identifies a target often someone perceived as vulnerable.
First the bully may create anonymous or fake accounts to avoid detection.
Next they initiate contact sometimes pretending to be friendly to gain trust.
Once engaged the bully gradually shifts to sending hurtful messages, spreading rumours or sharing embarrassing content publicly.
This behavior may escalate as others join in amplifying the harm through comments, shares or group chats.

Victims are often teenagers or young adults especially those who are socially isolated new to a community or different in appearance, behavior or beliefs.
However anyone can become a target.

The consequences can be severe.
Victims may experience anxiety, depression, low self-esteem and social withdrawal.
In extreme cases, cyberbullying has been linked to self harm or suicide.
Academicaly victims may see a decline in performance or avoid school altogether. 
For perpetrators consequences can include school disciplinary action or legal penalties.

This case highlights the importance of digital responsibility, awareness and timely intervention by parents, schools and authorities to prevent long term harm.
