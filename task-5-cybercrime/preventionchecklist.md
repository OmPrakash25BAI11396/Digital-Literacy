# Stay Safe Online – Checklist

## Basic Digital Hygiene
- Use strong, unique passwords for each account (mix of letters, numbers, symbols). Consider a password manager.
- Enable 2-factor authentication (2FA) on email, social media, and banking apps.

## Social Media & Privacy
- Limit what you share publicly (phone number, address, college ID, travel plans).
- Review privacy settings regularly on Instagram, WhatsApp, LinkedIn, etc.
- Don’t accept unknown friend requests—fake profiles are common in scams.

## Campus & Public Wi-Fi Safety
- Avoid logging into banking or sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi (cafés, campus hotspots).
- If necessary, use a trusted VPN or your mobile data instead.

## Phishing & Scams
- Verify links before clicking—look for spelling errors or strange URLs.
- Never share OTPs, passwords, or PINs—legitimate organizations will never ask for them.
- Be cautious of “urgent” messages about prizes, job offers, or account suspension.

##  UPI & Financial Safety (Important)
- Never approve a “Collect Request” in UPI unless you initiated it—receiving money does NOT require approval.
- Double-check UPI IDs before sending money—even a small typo can send money to the wrong person.
- Avoid scanning QR codes from unknown sources—they can redirect payments to fraudsters.
- Install apps only from official stores (Google Play / App Store) to avoid fake banking apps.		

## If Something Goes Wrong
- Act immediately if you suspect fraud—delay can make recovery harder.
- Report incidents on the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in)
- Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 for quick assistance in financial fraud cases.
